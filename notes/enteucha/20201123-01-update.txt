    CQZoneMatcherTestCase locks up if mem < 300g. Even though the host only has 240g free.

    CQZoneMatcherTestCase is very sensitive to zone density ratio for data input.
    As the overall density increases, the number of points per zone increases and the cost of inserting and indexing a data point increases.
    Result is inserting a level 12 [67,125,249] million row datasets can take ~1hr.

    Fix may be to have a 2D mapping, stripes within the zones ?

    Here is where HTMID has an advantage, because it is hierarchical, stepping down to smaller and smaller cells have less and less data in them.
    So the lowest level cells still have relatively small number of points in them.
    Can we map the cell density to show this ?
    A graph of number of cells against number of points in a cell, compared to the same for the zones.

    Working at the huge range of scales we may end up with almost all the data spread across a small number of zones.
    Worst case, all 67 million data points in one overcrowded zone.
